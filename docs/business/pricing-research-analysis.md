## Thorough Market Research - January 2026

## Executive Summary

Based on comprehensive research, the optimal pricing strategy for Nexus is:

| Tier | Price | Target | Rationale |
|------|-------|--------|-----------|
| **Starter** | $29/month | Testing/Individuals | Below $50 psychological barrier, captures price-sensitive |
| **Professional** | $99/month | Primary target | Just below $100 barrier, aligns with SMB budgets |
| **Business** | $249/month | Growing companies (10+) | Significant value vs agency costs |

**Key Finding:** $79 was too low. Research shows SMBs budget $50-500/month for AI automation, and our primary competitor Lindy charges $49.99-$299.99. Pricing at $99 positions Nexus as premium-but-accessible while maintaining healthy margins.

## Part 3: Pricing Psychology Research

### Critical Price Points (Psychological Barriers)

| Barrier | Perception | Nexus Strategy |
|---------|------------|----------------|
| **$50** | "Cheap tool, maybe not serious" | Starter tier at $29 (below barrier) |
| **$100** | "Professional tool, real investment" | Pro tier at $99 (just below barrier) |
| **$200** | "Business tool, needs approval" | Business tier at $249 (anchors value) |
| **$500** | "Enterprise, requires procurement" | Future tier only |

**Source:** [SaaS Pricing Psychology](https://www.getmonetizely.com/blogs/the-psychology-behind-price-points-that-drive-conversions-in-saas)

### Charm Pricing Effect

> "Pricing plans at $49 or $99 instead of $50 or $100 can make your offerings more attractive... Raising a price from $49 to $51 might seem minor, but it crosses a mental barrier, potentially reducing purchase likelihood."

**Nexus decision:** Use $29, $99, $249 (not $30, $100, $250)

### Three-Tier Optimization

> "Research from the University of California found that conversion rates are highest when customers are presented with three pricing tiers."
>
> "Studies show that tiered models increase conversions from free trials to paid plans by 27% when tiers are aligned with customer preferences."

**Source:** [Behavioral Pricing](https://www.getmonetizely.com/articles/behavioral-pricing-understanding-customer-psychology-in-saas)

### Anchoring Effect (Critical for Nexus)

> "McKinsey's research on SaaS pricing found that companies that successfully establish reference points can increase willingness to pay by up to 42%."

**How Nexus uses anchoring:**

Show the user:

## Part 8: Pricing Risks & Mitigations

### Risk 1: $99 Too High for Price-Sensitive SMBs

**Mitigation:**
- 14-day free trial (no credit card)
- ROI calculator shows agency savings
- Money-back guarantee first 30 days

### Risk 2: Lindy Drops Prices

**Mitigation:**
- Differentiate on flat pricing (their weakness)
- Build deeper integrations (their weakness)
- Focus on agency replacement messaging (not general AI)

### Risk 3: Zapier Adds AI at Low Price

**Mitigation:**
- Move FAST - establish brand before they launch
- Focus on SIMPLICITY - Zapier will bolt-on, not rebuild
- Target non-Zapier users (agency clients who never used Zapier)

### Final Pricing Recommendation

| Tier | Price | Change from Original |
|------|-------|---------------------|
| Starter | $29/month | No change |
| **Professional** | **$99/month** | **+$20 (+25%)** |

1. **$99 is still 96% cheaper than agencies** ($2,500/mo average)

3. **$99 positions as "professional"** not "cheap tool"
4. **$99 gives margin buffer** for unexpected costs
